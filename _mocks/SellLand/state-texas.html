Selling Land in Texas: What You Should Know

Texas is the largest contiguous state in the U.S., with over 170 million acres of diverse terrain— from Gulf Coast marshland to Hill Country ranches, West Texas desert to East Texas pine forests. This diversity means land values and buyer demand vary significantly by region.

Understanding Texas Land Markets

The Texas land market is segmented into distinct regions, each with its own buyer pool and pricing dynamics. Here's what sellers should know:

Central Texas / Hill Country

Highest demand, premium prices. Areas around Austin, San Antonio, and the Hill Country attract both investors and buyers seeking recreational land. Expect strong interest and quicker sales.

West Texas

Large acreage tracts at lower per-acre prices. Popular with hunters, solar/wind developers, and long-term investors. Longer sales cycles through traditional channels—but our investors buy here actively.

East Texas

Timberland and recreational properties. Strong demand for hunting land and weekend getaway parcels. Timber rights can add significant value.

South Texas / Valley

Agricultural land and ranch properties. Citrus groves, farmland, and ranches near the Rio Grande. Niche market with specialized buyers.

Texas-Specific Selling Considerations

Texas law is generally favorable to land sellers, but there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Property Taxes: Texas has no state income tax but higher property taxes. Back taxes can accumulate quickly on vacant land. Our investors can purchase land with outstanding taxes—they're deducted from your sale price.
  • Mineral Rights: In Texas, surface rights and mineral rights can be severed. If you own mineral rights, they can add value. If they were previously severed, it won't affect your sale.
  • Agricultural Exemptions: Land with "ag exempt" status has lower property taxes. Buyers often want to maintain this status; it's generally not an issue for cash sales.
  • Title Companies: Texas requires closings through a licensed title company or attorney. Our investors use established Texas title companies to ensure smooth closings.
