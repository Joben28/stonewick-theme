Visiting GracePoint

We want you to feel at home! When you arrive, greeters will welcome you and help you find your way. Services typically last about 75 minutes and include contemporary worship music, a practical message from the Bible, and time for prayer. Dress is casual — come as you are!

After the service, stop by our Welcome Center (look for the "I'm New" sign) to get a free gift and learn more about GracePoint.

We recommend arriving about 15 minutes early for your first visit. This gives you time to park, check in your kids (if applicable), grab a coffee, and find a seat without rushing. Our services start at 9:00 AM and 11:00 AM on Sundays.

We have a main parking lot adjacent to the building and an overflow lot across the street. First-time guests can use the designated "Guest Parking" spots near the main entrance. Parking volunteers will be available to direct you.

Yes! Our cafe opens 30 minutes before each service. We serve complimentary coffee, tea, and light refreshments. It's a great place to connect with others before or after the service.

Services & Worship

Sunday Services: 9:00 AM and 11:00 AM
Wednesday Night: 7:00 PM (midweek service with programs for all ages)

Special services are held on holidays — check our events page for details.

Our worship is contemporary Christian music led by a full band. We sing a mix of modern worship songs and updated hymns. The goal is to create an atmosphere where everyone can engage with God authentically.

Sunday services are typically 70-75 minutes. This includes about 25 minutes of worship, announcements, a 35-40 minute message, and a closing time of response.

Yes! We livestream our 11:00 AM Sunday service on our YouTube channel and Facebook page. Past sermons are available on our sermons page.

Kids & Youth

GracePoint Kids offers age-appropriate programs during all Sunday services and Wednesday nights:
  • Nursery: Birth - 2 years
  • Preschool: Ages 3-5
  • Elementary: K - 5th Grade

First-time families should visit the Kids Check-In desk in the main lobby. A team member will walk you through our secure registration process, which includes a digital check-in and matching security tags for you and your child. The process takes about 5 minutes your first time.

Child safety is our highest priority. All volunteers are background checked and trained. We use a secure check-in system with matching tags, maintain adult-to-child ratios, and follow strict safety protocols. Parents receive a pager/text if their child needs them during service.

GracePoint Youth is our ministry for 6th-12th graders. Middle school and high school students have their own gatherings on:
  • Sunday mornings: Special youth service during the 11:00 AM hour
  • Wednesday nights: 7:00 PM — games, worship, small groups

What We Believe

GracePoint is a non-denominational Christian church. We're part of the broader evangelical tradition and hold to the historic essentials of the Christian faith. We welcome people from all backgrounds and church traditions.

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. We believe the Bible is God's inspired Word and our guide for faith and life. We believe Jesus Christ is fully God and fully man, died for our sins, rose from the dead, and is coming again. Read our full statement of faith →

Yes! We practice believer's baptism by immersion as a public declaration of faith in Jesus Christ. We hold baptism services several times throughout the year. If you're interested in being baptized, let us know and we'll connect you with a pastor.

Yes, we celebrate communion (the Lord's Supper) on the first Sunday of each month. We practice open communion — all who have placed their faith in Jesus Christ are welcome to participate.

Getting Involved

Visit our Groups page to browse available groups and find one that fits your schedule and interests. You can also contact us and we'll help match you with a group. New groups launch each semester.

Membership involves attending our "Starting Point" class, meeting with a pastor, and signing our membership covenant. Visit our membership page for details and to sign up for the next class.

We have serving opportunities for everyone! From greeting to kids ministry to production to outreach. Check out our volunteer page to see all the options and fill out an interest form.

You can submit a prayer request on our prayer page, call our prayer line at (555) 123-PRAY, or speak with someone on our prayer team after any service. All requests are treated with care and confidentiality.

Giving

We offer several giving options:
  • Online: Through our giving page (one-time or recurring)
  • Text: Text any amount to 84321
  • App: Through the GracePoint mobile app
  • In Person: Offering boxes at sanctuary entrances
  • Mail: Send checks to our office

Yes, GracePoint is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization. All donations are tax-deductible. You'll receive a year-end giving statement for your records.

Yes, you can designate your gift to specific funds like Missions, Youth Ministry, Building Fund, or Benevolence. Select your preferred fund when giving online or write it on your check.

Practical Information

Yes, our facility is fully ADA accessible. We have accessible parking, ramps, elevators, accessible restrooms, and wheelchair seating areas in the sanctuary. Let us know if you need any accommodations.

Yes, hearing assistance devices are available at the welcome desk. We also have a hearing loop system in the sanctuary for those with compatible hearing aids.

Our facility is available for weddings, memorial services, and other approved events. Contact our events team at events@gracepoint.church for availability and pricing.

Our Benevolence Ministry exists to help those facing financial hardship. We can assist with food, utilities, rent, and other urgent needs. Contact our care team confidentially through our contact page or call the church office.
